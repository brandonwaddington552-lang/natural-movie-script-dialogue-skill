# Natural Movie Script Dialogue Techniques

## Speech patterns that feel real on screen

- Interruptions and overlaps: "I told you—" / "No, you didn't. You never—"
- Trailing off or self-correction: "It was the— look, it doesn't matter."
- Softeners under pressure that still land hard: "I mean… maybe we just don't."
- Minimal reactive lines that carry weight: "Yeah.", "Sure.", "Right.", "Okay."
- Questions that aren't really questions: "You good with that?"
- Loaded silence and beats instead of filling every gap.

## Character voice fingerprints

Give each speaker clear, consistent habits:
- One character speaks in short, clipped sentences.
- Another runs long and circles the point.
- Someone defaults to humor or deflection when cornered.
- Someone else uses specific slang, profession-specific terms, or regional flavor without turning into caricature.
- What a character refuses to say is often more revealing than what they do say.

## Common AI tells to eliminate

- Characters announcing their internal state ("I'm so angry right now")
- Perfect grammatical completeness under emotional stress
- Every line advancing plot with zero subtext
- Symmetrical back-and-forth that feels timed
- Dialogue that could be cut-and-pasted into an essay
- Explicit theme statements or moral takeaways
- Over-explaining relationships or history the audience can infer

## Micro-pattern examples

Bad (on-the-nose / expositional):
```
SARAH
I'm really hurt that you lied to me about the money. 
It makes me feel like I can't trust you anymore.
```

Better (natural, subtextual):
```
SARAH
You said the account was empty.

DAVID
It is.

SARAH
(beat)
There's a deposit from last Tuesday.
```

Bad (perfect turn-taking):
```
JOHN
How was your day?

MARY
It was fine. How was yours?

JOHN
Also fine. Want to talk about the promotion?
```

Better (interruption + avoidance):
```
JOHN
How was—

MARY
Don't.

JOHN
I was just asking about your day.

MARY
And I said don't.
```

Bad (monologue dump):
```
DETECTIVE
I've been a cop for twenty years and I've seen a lot of things, 
but this case is different because it reminds me of my own 
daughter and the night she disappeared and I never found her.
```

Better (implication + restraint):
```
DETECTIVE
Twenty years.

He looks at the photo on the board a second too long.

DETECTIVE
You don't walk away from some of them.
```

## Formatting reminders

- Action lines describe only what the camera can see or hear.
- Avoid directing the actor's internal life ("she feels betrayed").
- Parentheticals are for delivery that isn't clear from context: (quietly), (overlapping), (to herself).
- White space is your friend. Short lines and beats create rhythm.
